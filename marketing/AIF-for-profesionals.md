# AI Work Automation — Program modules

_Stop doing it manually. Start automating it intelligently._

**4 Weeks · 1 Live Class/Week · No Coding Required**

---

## Curriculum

### How AI Really Works

`LLMs` `Prompt Fundamentals` `Mental Models` `Job Audit`

Build the mental model that makes everything else click. You will understand what language models actually are, why choosing the right model matters for cost and quality, and audit your own job to identify your top automation candidates — the foundation every other week builds on.

**Self-paced — complete before live class:**

- What LLMs are and how they work in plain language
- Why model size vs. task match matters — cost, speed, and quality
- The difference between a chatbot, an assistant, and an agent
- Job audit exercise: list your 10 most repetitive weekly tasks
- Prompt fundamentals: role, context, format, constraints

**Live class — 1 session, applying what you learned:**

- Apply your job audit — identify your top 3 automation candidates
- Write your first 5 prompts for real tasks you do every week
- Q&A and peer sharing: what tasks are people automating?

---

### Prompting as a Skill

`Prompt Patterns` `Chain of Thought` `Few-Shot` `Prompt Library` `Markdown`

The better your prompts, the less work you do. You will move beyond basic instructions into reusable prompt patterns — chain of thought, few-shot, persona, structured output — and leave with a personal prompt library that works for your actual job tasks.

**Self-paced — complete before live class:**

- Prompt patterns: chain of thought, few-shot, persona, structured output
- Prompting for emails, reports, research, and meeting summaries
- Markdown basics — what it is, when AI uses it, how to control it
- How to manage AI context: what the model remembers and what it forgets
- Building a personal prompt library in Notion or Google Docs

**Live class — 1 session, applying what you learned:**

- Workshop: refine your prompts live with instructor feedback
- Leave with 3–5 working prompts for your actual job tasks
- Peer review: swap prompts and test each other's

---

### Your Personal AI Assistant

`OpenClaw` `Agent Skills` `JSON Basics` `Tool Integration`

On-demand automation for the tasks you trigger yourself. You will install OpenClaw, run your first community skill, and create a custom skill from a prompt template — then connect your assistant to the tools you already use every day.

**Self-paced — complete before live class:**

- What AI agents are and how skills give them specialized behavior
- Installing OpenClaw and running your first community skill
- JSON basics — reading and editing configuration files in context
- Creating your own skill from a prompt template
- Connecting OpenClaw to Google Drive, Gmail, or Notion

**Live class — 1 session, applying what you learned:**

- Live demo: instructor builds a skill from scratch
- Hands-on: every student runs a skill on a real personal task
- Troubleshooting session — get unstuck on your setup

---

### Automation Pipelines with n8n

`n8n` `Nodes & Triggers` `Webhooks` `AI Integration` `Final Project`

Build systems that run without you ever triggering them. You will connect n8n to your existing tools, link it to an AI agent for smart decision-making steps, and ship your final automation — a real working system you own and can demo to anyone.

**Self-paced — complete before live class:**

- n8n fundamentals: nodes, triggers, and sequential workflows
- Connecting n8n to Gmail, Slack, Google Sheets, and webhooks
- Linking n8n to an AI agent for decision-making steps inside a workflow
- Final project prep: map and build your automation

**Live class — 1 session, applying what you learned:**

- Final project demos: every student presents their working automation
- Live feedback from instructor and peers
- Next steps: what to build next and where to go deeper

## Capstone

### AI-Automated Workflow

`Final Project` `Real Automation` `Portfolio`

The course closes with a real deliverable: a working automation that handles one of your most painful recurring tasks. You will present what it does, what problem it solves, and demo it live — your first AI portfolio piece, something concrete you built and can show to anyone.

**Deliverable components:**

- A working n8n automation solving a real personal or professional task
- At least one AI decision-making step integrated into the workflow
- Live demo presented to instructor and peers in the final class
